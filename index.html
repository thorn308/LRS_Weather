 Help & Glossary

Wind 10m vs Wind 80m

10m (Surface) is the wind at ground level — what you feel standing at your shooting position. This is what deflects your bullet just after it leaves the muzzle.

80m (Aloft) is the wind roughly 260 ft above ground. For long-range shots, your bullet travels through this layer for most of its flight. Surface calm with a strong 80m wind is a deceptive setup — your bullet can drift significantly even when it feels still on the ground.

Crosswind & Headwind/Tailwind

Set your shooting azimuth (direction you're firing toward, in degrees) and a new column appears in the table showing the wind broken into two components:

X-Wind — the crosswind component (L or R). This is the number that matters most for horizontal bullet drift. Higher = more correction needed.

H/T — headwind (into your face) or tailwind (behind you). A headwind slightly increases bullet stability; a tailwind can reduce it. Effect is smaller than crosswind but noticeable at extreme range.

Density Altitude

Density Altitude (DA) is the altitude your bullet "thinks" it's flying at, based on actual air pressure and temperature. Hot, low-pressure days produce high DA — thinner air means less drag, so bullets travel flatter and hit higher than your zero. Cold, high-pressure days produce low DA — denser air means more drag.

A DA change of 3,000–5,000 ft can shift your point of impact by several inches at 1,000 yards. Always check DA vs your zero conditions before a long-range session.

Color guide: muted = <2,000ft  ·  amber = 2–4K  ·  orange = 4–7K  ·  red = 7K+

Mirage Indicator

Mirage appears when you see heat shimmer through your optic. It forms when the ground is hot, humidity is low, skies are clear, and wind is calm. While mirage can obscure your target, experienced shooters also read it to judge wind speed and direction — slow boil = calm, fast lean = stronger wind.

The app flags mirage when: temp >70°F, RH <40%, cloud cover <20%, and wind <5 mph.

Dew Point

The dew point is the temperature at which moisture condenses. When the dew point is close to the air temperature (within ~5°F), fog, frost, or scope fogging becomes likely. A low dew point means dry, clear air. High dew point (above 65°F) means muggy conditions that can fog scopes and affect glass clarity.

Barometric Pressure

Pressure directly affects air density and therefore bullet trajectory. Standard sea-level pressure is 29.92 inHg. Lower pressure = thinner air = less drag = bullets hit higher. Higher pressure = denser air = more drag = bullets hit lower. The Density Altitude number already accounts for this combined with temperature, so use DA as your ballistics input.

Shooting Conditions Score

Each hour is automatically rated based on wind, precipitation probability, and visibility:

EXCELLENT Wind <5 mph · Rain <10% · Vis >5mi

GOOD Wind <10 mph · Rain <25% · Vis >3mi

FAIR Wind <15 mph · Rain <50% · Vis >1.2mi

POOR Conditions exceed any of the above thresholds

Lunar Phase

Moon phase is shown in each day's summary. For dawn/dusk shoots, a full or gibbous moon provides natural light. For night precision work, a new moon = full darkness. The illumination percentage tells you how much of the moon's face is lit.

Wind Direction Arrows

Arrows show the direction the wind is traveling toward (not from). An arrow pointing right means wind is moving left-to-right across your position — which pushes your bullet to the right. The cardinal label (N, SW, etc.) and degree value show the direction the wind is coming from (standard meteorological convention).

 Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

Data We Collect

LRS Weather does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data on external servers. All data you enter — saved locations, saved forecasts, preferences — is stored exclusively on your device using your browser's local storage.

Location Data

If you use the "Use My Location" feature, your coordinates are sent directly to the Open-Meteo weather API and the Nominatim geocoding service to retrieve your forecast. Your location is never stored on any LRS Weather server — we don't operate one.

Third-Party Services

Open-Meteo — weather data. See their privacy policy at open-meteo.com.

OpenStreetMap / Nominatim — map tiles and geocoding. See nominatim.org for their policy.

Esri — satellite imagery layer. See esri.com/legal/privacy for their policy.

Device Storage

The app uses localStorage on your device to remember your saved locations, saved forecasts, and settings (unit preferences, onboarding status). This data never leaves your device. You can clear it at any time through your browser or device settings.

Contact

Questions about privacy? Open an issue at the project repository or contact the developer through the App Store listing.
